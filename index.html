About Me

Welcome to my Portfolio!

I define myself as a learning enthusiast. I get intellectually motivated when I am challenged by things that I do not know. In May 2016, I graduated from University of Texas at Austin (UT) with my Ph.D. in English and Comparative Literature, a path of education that allowed me to become the intellectual that I claim to be today.

As a newly minted doctor, I finally have the time to learn things that are not in my comfort zone (yet). I have started learning how to code in UT Coding Boot Camp to complement my soft skills with technical ones so that in my professional life I can employ both. I am starting a Masters degree in computer science at Stanford University in the Fall. I am thrilled to become a computer scientist with a strong humanities background, which I believe is a perfect combination!

This page will serve as a brief introduction and proof of progress for my initial experiments with coding, which I often tend to define as creating. In the near future, I aim to excel my programming skills and use them mainly to support non-profits. I am a big supporter of open access. One of the projects that I am currently working on is writing a book proposal for the "Coding for Humanists" book series by Texas A&M, which aims to serve scholars who are working in the field of digital humanities. The proposal will cover a holistic approach to working with databases, such as relational databases (which will include a treatment of SQL), graph databases (which could include treatments of Neo4j and SPARQL), and enterprise search platforms (which could include treatments of Solr and Apache Lucene).

Finally, I am more than a humanist coder! In addition to teaching and learning new stuff, I enjoy working out, particularly weight lifting and road biking.
